(h) Care for Young Converts

Concerning them emphatically should be received the charge, “ Feed my lambs,” which Christ gave so impressively and in such memorable circumstances. What they will be in the future as to devoted piety and useful Christian life depends very much upon the attention which is given to them now. If, as babes in Christ, they are not carefully trained now, they may very soon backslide and become mere ciphers in the church. What is to be done for them so as to help them on in the way of spiritual progress is one of the most perplexing problems with which the pastor has to do. It often tries him greatly to determine what will be really practicable in assisting not only to keep them in their first love, but also lead to their growth in grace and usefulness. Here the counsels of experience, gathered up from many pastors and many years, are needed.
