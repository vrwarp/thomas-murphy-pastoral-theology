It is to be greatly lamented that, whilst it is their special calling, and they have every opportunity of knowing, and so much depends on it, many of our ministers are so poorly informed about the missionary and other benevolent operations of the day. It must surely be through inadvertence that they allow it to be so. Shall the watchmen know but little of the movements of either friends or foes to that cause in which they are enlisted? Shall the stewards be ignorant of the vast interests which their King has committed to their trust? Is not ignorance on the part of pastors here both a sin and a disgrace ?

(b) The Pastor Should Communicate this Information to the People

He should preach on the obligation and magnitude of the general benevolent work of the Church, upon the important and detailed operations of each branch of it, and upon its present condition and wants. He should incidentally introduce into his sermons and addresses matters pertaining to the cause. He should keep the people informed by reading the deliverances of the Church courts concerning collections and other duties connected with the furtherance of the gospel; and he should encourage them to inform themselves, and direct them in the study of a subject which is the grandest that can engage the thoughts of man.
