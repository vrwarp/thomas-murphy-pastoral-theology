The great study should be how all these can be brought to lend their aid in making the preaching more effective.

The minister ought, by all means, to cultivate a very high appreciation of the importance of preaching, and to concentrate on it all his energies. The hour in the pulpit he should look upon as the harvest hour of the week. He should cherish a growing assurance that his ministrations there will tell most powerfully upon men, and that to proclaim the messages of God to man is the most noble and solemn thing to which a man can be called. Such exalted estimate of what it is to preach would help to the better performance of the service, and often prevent it from being slighted. The preacher should strive to work himself up to admiration and love for this greatest duty of his office.
